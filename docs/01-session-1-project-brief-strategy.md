# Session 1 — Project Brief + Landing Page Strategy

## Project
Nano Line Healthy Bread Production Line  
Model: **NL-RM210 / Steel Master**

---

## 1. Project Positioning

This landing page should position the **NL-RM210 / Steel Master** as a custom industrial production solution for healthy bread manufacturing — not as a fixed ready-made machine.

The core message is:

> Nano Line helps bakeries and food manufacturers build a healthy bread production line tailored to their required capacity, loaf size, loaf weight, loaf shape, automation level, and factory setup.

The page should make the client feel that the line is engineered around their project, not simply sold as a standard machine.

---

## 2. Strategic Goal of the Landing Page

The main goal is not to display a price or close the sale instantly.

The main goal is to qualify serious B2B leads and give the sales team enough technical and commercial information to prepare a short customized quotation after understanding the client’s needs.

The page should help the client understand:

- What the line does
- Why it is customizable
- What production range it can support
- What technical decisions affect the final configuration
- Why price cannot be fixed publicly
- What information is needed before receiving a quotation
- Why manufacturing requires time and is not immediate delivery

## 6. Key Differentiators

### 1. Customizable Line Configuration
The line can be adapted based on production capacity, loaf shape, loaf size, loaf weight, and add-on requirements.

### 2. Flexible Bread Format
The page should clearly communicate that the line can support round or square bread depending on configuration.

### 3. Scalable Production
The line is suitable for industrial output and can be configured based on the required hourly and daily production volume.

### 4. Complete Production Flow
The line covers the full process from dough dividing, proofing, rounding, arranging, sheeting, forming, baking, and cooling.

### 5. Optional Add-ons
Mixing, weighing, packing, sealing, counting, and air compressor systems can be added depending on the project scope.

### 6. Sales-Ready Calculator
The calculator should help the client estimate their required hourly production and help sales understand the correct configuration direction.

## 7. Customer Objections & Page Response

### Objection 1: “What is the price?”
Answer:  
The price depends on the required capacity, loaf specifications, add-ons, site requirements, and installation scope. Therefore, Nano Line prepares a customized quotation after reviewing the project details.

### Objection 2: “Is this line suitable for my product?”
Answer:  
The line is configurable for different loaf sizes, weights, and shapes. The client can submit their product requirements for technical review.

### Objection 3: “Can I produce square bread, not only round bread?”
Answer:  
Yes. The page should clearly mention round or square bread options based on configuration.

### Objection 4: “Can the production capacity be increased?”
Answer:  
Yes. The line can be configured and studied based on the required output. Higher-capacity requirements may need customized configuration or a multi-line production study.

### Objection 5: “How quickly can I receive the line?”
Answer:  
This is a built-to-order industrial line, not immediate stock delivery. Average manufacturing and preparation lead time is approximately 120–180 days, depending on final configuration.

### Objection 6: “What do I need to prepare in my factory?”
Answer:  
The page should include a site readiness section covering space, electricity, gas or diesel, ventilation, compressed air, maintenance access, and trained operators.

## 11. Calculator Strategy

The calculator is a qualification tool, not a pricing tool.

### Inputs

- Required loaves per day
- Working hours per day
- Target loaf weight
- Loaf shape
- Target loaf size
- Monthly working days
- Mixing system required?
- Packaging support required?

### Outputs

- Required hourly production
- Estimated monthly production
- Suggested configuration direction
- Suggested add-ons
- CTA to request a custom quotation

### Recommendation Logic

If required hourly production is less than or equal to 3,500:

- Standard configuration may be suitable.

If required hourly production is between 3,500 and 6,000:

- Custom configuration is recommended based on loaf size, weight, and shape.

If required hourly production is above 6,000:

- Higher-capacity customization or multi-line production study is recommended.

## 12. Content Rules

### Must Do

- Use B2B industrial language
- Focus on customization and technical review
- Make the page visually rich
- Explain why pricing is not fixed
- Present manufacturing lead time as approximate
- Use “built-to-order” clearly
- Push users toward calculator and lead form

### Must Avoid

- Do not show prices
- Do not mention fixed quotation values
- Do not promise exact delivery time
- Do not claim instant delivery
- Do not overpromise production capacity without saying it depends on configuration
- Do not use low-quality PDF screenshots directly without redesign
- Do not make the page look like a downloadable brochure

---

## 13. Brand / Tone Direction

The tone should feel:

- Industrial
- Premium
- Technical
- Trustworthy
- Clear
- Sales-focused
- Not too emotional
- Not too academic

The page should feel like a modern B2B manufacturing website, not a retail product page.

---

## 14. Final Strategic Summary

This landing page should work as a sales enablement tool.

It should educate the client, explain the customization logic, qualify the lead, and prepare the sales team to send a short customized quotation.

The strongest angle is:

> Tell us what bread you want to produce and how much output you need — we will configure the production line around your project.
